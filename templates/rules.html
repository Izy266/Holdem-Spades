How to play Texas Hold'em

Step-by-step instructions to master the game

Rules

Texas Hold'em is a popular variation of poker. The game consists of two cards being dealt face down to each player and then five community cards being placed face-up by the dealer—a series of three ("the flop") then two additional single cards ("the turn" and "the river"). Players have the options to check, bet, or fold after each deal.

Hand Rankings

The objective of Texas Hold'em is to make the best five-card hand you can, using a combination of the two 'hole cards' you are dealt and the five community cards on the board. A straight for example is a 5 card hand in which all the card ranks are sequential with the lowest straight consisting of cards with ranks of A, 2, 3, 4, 5 and the highest straight, the Royal Straight, consisting of cards with ranks of 10, J, Q, K, A in that sequential order. An (A)ce can be the lowest or highest card of a straight, but a straight can not "wrap around"; a hand with a (J)ack, (Q)ueen, (K)ing, (A)ce, deuce(2) for example would not be considered a straight. With hands of the same ranking, the tie is broken by comparing the highest ranking card in each hand, if the hands have the same highest ranking card, the tie is broken by comparing the second highest ranking card and so on. Order of Play

After each hand, the dealer button moves one position clockwise, which shifts the small blind, big blind, and subsequent positions for the next hand. This rotation ensures that each player has an opportunity to act as the dealer. The community cards are dealt in stages, with each stage having its own betting round. The stages are:

  • Pre-flop: Each player receives two private hole cards.
  • Flop: Three community cards are dealt face-up in the center of the table.
  • Turn: A fourth community card is dealt face-up.
  • River: The fifth and final community card is dealt face-up.

Betting Process

Blinds: The game starts with two players posting forced bets known as the "small blind" and the "big blind." The player to the left of the dealer posts the small blind, and the player to their left posts the big blind. The blinds are predetermined amounts, and they initiate the betting. Each player is then dealt two private cards, known as "hole cards" or "pocket cards."

Minimum Raise: When a player chooses to raise, the minimum amount they can raise is typically double the current bet or the big blind. For example, if the current bet or big blind is $10, the minimum raise would be $20.

Maximum Bet: In no-limit Texas Hold'em, there is no maximum limit on the amount a player can bet or raise during a betting round. A player can go all-in, betting all their remaining chips.

All-In: If a player does not have enough chips to match a bet or raise, they can go all-in by betting their remaining chips. In this case, a separate side pot may be created for the remaining players who can continue betting.

First Betting Round (Pre-Flop): Starting with the player to the left of the big blind, each player has the option to call (match the big blind), raise (increase the bet), or fold (discard their hand and forfeit the round). Betting continues clockwise. After the first betting round, the flop is dealt.

Second Betting Round (Post-Flop): Starting with the player to the left of the dealer, a new betting round begins. Players can check (pass the action to the next player if no one has bet), bet, call, raise, or fold, depending on the previous actions. After the second betting round, the turn is dealt.

Third Betting Round (Post-Turn): Another betting round occurs, starting with the player to the left of the dealer. The options are the same as in the previous rounds. Following the third betting round, the river is dealt.

Final Betting Round (Post-River): The last betting round takes place, starting with the player to the left of the dealer. Players can make their final bets or folds. If there are two or more players remaining after the final betting round, a showdown occurs.

Showdown

Showdown Obligation: In a showdown, all players involved in the hand who don't wish to forfeit the pot are obligated to reveal their hole cards to determine the best hand. If there is only one player remaining after the final betting round, that player is not required to show their cards.

Order of Revealing Cards: The player who made the last aggressive action (the last bet or raise) is the first to reveal their cards. If there was no bet on the final betting round, the player to the left of the dealer button is the first to show.

Mucking (Not Showing) Cards: Players have the option to muck (not show) their cards if they have a losing hand.

All-In: If a player does not have enough chips to match a bet or raise, they can go all-in by betting their remaining chips. In this case, a separate side pot may be created for the remaining players who can continue betting.

Side Pots: In situations where one or more players are all-in with different chip stacks, multiple side pots may be created. Each side pot is contested by the players involved in that particular pot. The main pot is awarded to the player with the best hand among all players.

Split Pots: If two or more players have the same winning hand, the pot is split equally among them. It's possible for a hand to be split between the main pot and one or more side pots.

Tips for Beginners

  • Start by playing low stakes games, familiarize yourself with the rules and gameplay.
  • Pay attention to your position at the table. Being in a later position allows you to see how others act before you make decisions.
  • Observe your opponents and adapt your strategy accordingly. Look for patterns in their playing styles.
  • Don't feel pressured to play every hand. Patience is a virtue in poker, and waiting for good opportunities can be more profitable.
  • Bluff sparingly and choose your spots wisely. Bluffing is more effective when done selectively.
  • Don't be afraid to fold a hand if it's not strong. Knowing when to cut your losses is a crucial skill in poker.
